Not just a downloader

The things classic clients can't do.

TorrentHunt is built on the BitTorrent ↔ WebRTC bridge — so a torrent isn't just something you download, it's something you can watch, share and sync, peer-to-peer.

Watch anywhere

Stream a torrent while it's still downloading — to your phone, laptop or TV. Even formats the browser can't play, transcoded on the fly.

Cast to TV

Find Chromecast and Android TV devices on your Wi-Fi and play a torrent on the big screen, with pause, resume and stop.

Share to a browser

Send a finished download straight into a friend's browser over a link — no install, no account on their side. Pure WebRTC.

Private friend rooms

Invite-only rooms where everyone's files auto-sync into a shared folder and you chat — end-to-end encrypted and cryptographically signed.

Privacy dashboard

See the public IP peers connect to, your ISP and VPN status at a glance, with IP-leak detection and a VPN kill-switch.

Bring your own indexers

Plug in Jackett, Prowlarr or a custom API, and add your own RSS feeds. No indexers are bundled — you stay in control of your sources.

Friend swarms

Private rooms that need no infrastructure.

Spin up a room, share a speakable code, and your group's files distribute peer-to-peer — with encrypted chat and connectivity that hops between members so it works across home networks.

Works across networks — with zero servers

Direct, IPv6 and STUN cover most connections. When two members can't reach each other, a third member relays the (still end-to-end encrypted) traffic between them — so a room works without any infrastructure of its own.

Privacy by default

You can see exactly what the swarm sees.

Privacy isn't a checkbox buried in settings — it's a live dashboard, and everything sensitive is encrypted on your own machine.

Live exposure dashboard

The public IP peers actually connect to, your ISP, location and VPN status — colour-coded, refreshed only on demand.

IP-leak detection

Warns when the IP facing the swarm looks like a consumer ISP rather than your VPN — catch a leak before you download.

VPN kill-switch

Auto-pauses every torrent the moment your VPN drops, plus a check at startup. Your real IP doesn't leak by accident.

Secrets encrypted at rest

Provider logins, room keys and chat history are encrypted with OS-level storage (DPAPI / Keychain / libsecret). No telemetry, ever.

Questions

The honest FAQ.

Is it really free?
Yes — free and open source under the MIT license. There's no paid tier, no accounts, and no telemetry.
Do you run any servers?
No. Everything runs on your machine and directly between you and your peers. The only shared infrastructure is the public WebRTC rendezvous trackers used to help peers find each other — they never see your files or any plaintext.
Do my files go through the cloud?
No. Streaming, sharing and rooms are all peer-to-peer. In rooms, traffic is end-to-end encrypted, so even a member relaying for others only ever forwards ciphertext.
How do rooms stay private?
The invite code derives an AES-256-GCM key, so only people with the code can read anything. Chat messages are additionally signed with a per-device key, so even a code holder can't post under another member's identity, and the history is encrypted on disk.
Is this legal?
BitTorrent is a protocol, and TorrentHunt bundles no indexers for copyrighted material — you bring your own sources and are responsible for what you download and share. Use it for legal content.
macOS and Linux?
The app is built cross-platform (Electron), and macOS / Linux builds are planned. Today the published installer is Windows only.
Legal use only. TorrentHunt does not bundle indexers for copyrighted material. Any search providers or feeds are added by you, and you are responsible for what you download and share.
